About

intersection

I'm a senior at Syracuse University's Maxwell School of Citizenship and Public Affairs, graduating May 2026 with a degree in International Relations and minors in Psychology and Applied Data Analytics — part of the Renée Crown University Honors Program.

My work sits at the intersection of behavioral theory, organizational dynamics, and quantitative analysis. I'm drawn to questions that conventional frameworks leave partially answered: why do high-performing institutions fail under pressure, how do decision-makers respond when incentives and information conflict, and what historical precedents reveal about systemic risk.

My honors thesis examines Gandhi's assassination and Godse's political reasoning as a lens for understanding how the Indian state consolidated power in 1947–48 — an argument about how crisis accelerates institutional change rather than disrupting it.

Mixed-Methods Research Strategic Analysis
2025 · Syracuse University

AI as a Mental Health Aid: Is ChatGPT a Viable Therapeutic Tool?

Led a multi-phase research project examining whether ChatGPT can serve as a viable mental health aid. Conducted live behavioral observations and semi-structured interviews with 8 participants, performing thematic analysis to identify where AI creates value versus where it fails users. Individually designed two experimental frameworks — an RCT and a Micro-Randomized Trial — to test hypotheses around emotional impact. Key recommendation: a hybrid human-AI care model offers the highest-viability path for closing mental health access gaps, with AI positioned for triage and emotional regulation, not therapeutic replacement.

IST 414 · Qualitative Research · Experimental Design · Thematic Analysis
Policy Analysis Crisis Strategy
2025 · Maxwell School

Senkaku/Diaoyu Crisis: Dual-Track De-Escalation Strategy

Simulated as China's Ministry of Foreign Affairs team, developed a structured policy response to a maritime collision near the disputed Senkaku/Diaoyu Islands that resulted in the detention of thirteen Chinese fishermen. Proposed a dual-track strategy combining firm diplomatic protest with a cooperative crisis-management mechanism — balancing sovereignty claims, domestic nationalist pressure, and the risk of military escalation with Japan and the United States. Presented to faculty panel with full justification of policy tools, trade-offs, and alternatives considered.

Foreign Affairs Simulation · Diplomatic Strategy · IR Theory
Independent Research Psychology
2022 · Lumiere Education

ODD/CD in Youth as Predictors of Adult Criminal Behavior

Conducted an independent literature review examining the psychological link between Oppositional Defiant Disorder, Conduct Disorder, and future criminal activity — exploring how childhood behavioral disorders, parenting dynamics, and systemic failures in criminal justice interact. Mentored by a researcher from the University of Oxford. Presented findings at the Lumiere Research Symposium. Argues that legal frameworks must evolve to account for mental health diagnosis in assessing criminal responsibility.
