About

Thinking at the
intersection

I'm a senior at Syracuse University's Maxwell School of Citizenship and Public Affairs, graduating May 2026 with a degree in International Relations and minors in Psychology and Applied Data Analytics — part of the Renée Crown University Honors Program.

My work sits at the intersection of political systems, behavioral theory, and quantitative analysis. I'm drawn to questions that conventional frameworks leave partially answered: why do high-performing institutions fail under pressure, how does political context distort market signals, and what historical precedents tell us about state behavior in crisis.

My honors thesis examines Gandhi's assassination and Godse's political reasoning as a lens for understanding how the Indian state consolidated power in 1947–48 — an argument that violence can function as political accelerant rather than rupture.
